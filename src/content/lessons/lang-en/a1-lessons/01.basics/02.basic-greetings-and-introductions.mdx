# Basic Greetings and Introductions

## Greetings for Different Times of Day

### Formal Greetings:

- **Jó reggelt!** - Good morning! (until ~9 AM)
- **Jó napot!** - Good afternoon! (9:00 AM - 6:00 PM)
- **Jó estét!** - Good evening! (after 6:00 PM)
- **Jó éjszakát!** - Good night! (before sleep)

### Informal Greetings:

- **Szia!** - Hi! / Bye! (to one person)
- **Sziasztok!** - Hi! / Bye! (to multiple people)
- **Helló!** - Hello! (universal)
- **Szervusz!** - Hello! / Hi! (old-fashioned, but still used)

::info

Hungarians very clearly distinguish between formal and informal communication. With strangers, elderly people, and superiors, always use formal greetings!

::

## How to Say Goodbye

### Formal:

- **Viszontlátásra!** - Goodbye!
- **Viszlát!** - Goodbye! (short form)
- **Viszonthallásra!** - Goodbye! / Speak soon! (on the phone)

### Informal:

- **Szia!** / **Sziasztok!** - Bye! / Bye-bye!
- **Szervusz!** - Bye!
- **Puszi!** - Kisses! (very informal, between friends)
- **Pápá!** - Bye-bye! (very informal, not used as often)

### Other Useful Phrases:

- **Viszlát később!** - See you later!
- **Holnap találkozunk!** - See you tomorrow!
- **Jó utat!** - Have a safe trip! (literally: good road)
- **Szép napot!** - Have a nice day!

## Introductions - The Basics

### How to Introduce Yourself:

**Formal:**

- **A nevem...** - My name is...
- **Az én nevem...** - My name is... (emphasized)

**Informal:**

- **...vagyok** - I am...
- **Én ...** - I am...

### Examples:

- **A nevem Anna.** - My name is Anna.
- **Péter vagyok.** - I am Péter.
- **Én Katalin vagyok.** - I am Katalin.

::tip

In Hungarian, it's not mandatory to use the personal pronoun "én" (I), because the verb already indicates the person. You can simply say: "Anna vagyok."

::

## Questions When Meeting Someone

### How to Ask for a Name:

**Formal:**

- **Mi a neve?** - What is your name? (to one person)
- **Hogy hívják?** - What is your name? (literally: How do they call you?)

**Informal:**

- **Mi a neved?** - What is your name? (to one person)
- **Hogy hívnak?** - What is your name? (literally: How do they call you?)

### Responses:

- **A nevem...** - My name is...
- **Engem...-nak/nek hívnak** - My name is... (literally: I am called...)

### Example Dialogue:

- **A:** Jó napot! Mi a neve? //Good afternoon! What is your name?//
- **B:** Jó napot! A nevem Kovács János. És önt? //Good afternoon! My name is János Kovács. And you?//
- **A:** Szabó Péter vagyok. Örülök, hogy megismerhetem. //I am Péter Szabó. Nice to meet you.//
- **B:** Én is örülök. //Nice to meet you too.//

## Polite Phrases

### Must-Learn Phrases:

- **Köszönöm** - Thank you
- **Kérem** - Please / You're welcome (when offering something)
- **Igen** - Yes
- **Nem** - No
- **Elnézést** - Excuse me (to get attention or apologize for a minor issue)
- **Bocsánat** - Sorry / Excuse me (to apologize)
- **Szívesen** - You're welcome (in response to thank you)
- **Nincs mit** - Don't mention it / No problem

### Additional Phrases:

- **Örülök, hogy megismerhetem** - Nice to meet you
- **Nagyon szépen köszönöm** - Thank you very much
- **Kérem szépen** - Please (more polite)

::warning

- **Köszönöm** - universal "thank you"
- **Köszönöm szépen** - more polite
- **Nagyon szépen köszönöm** - very polite

Use them according to the situation!

::

## How to Ask "How are you?"

**Formal:**

- **Hogy van?** - How are you? (to one person)
- **Hogy vannak?** - How are you? (to multiple people)

**Informal:**

- **Hogy vagy?** - How are you?
- **Mi újság?** - What's new?
- **Mizujs?** - What's new? (shortened, more slangy)
- **Hogy ityeg? (a fityeg)** - How it's hangin'? (very informal, somewhat outdated slang)

### Responses:

**Positive:**

- **Jól, köszönöm** - Good, thank you
- **Nagyon jól** - Very well
- **Köszönöm, jól. És ön/te?** - Thank you, I'm fine. And you?

**Neutral:**

- **Rendben** - Alright / Okay
- **Úgy ahogy** - So-so (literally: as it is)

**Negative:**

- **Nem túl jól** - Not too well
- **Rosszul** - Badly / Not well

::tip

Rendben can also mean "okay" or "all right".

For example:

- **Vásárolj be, légyszi** - Do the grocery shopping, please
- **Rendben (van)** - Okay / All right

That's why sometimes people add "vagyok" to the response to "Hogy vagy?" to be more precise.

::

## Full Introduction Dialogues

### Informal:

- **A:** Szia! Én Péter vagyok. Te?
- **B:** Szia! Anna vagyok. Örülök!
- **A:** Én is. Hogy vagy?
- **B:** Jól vagyok, köszönöm. És te?
- **A:** Köszönöm, szintén jól.

### Formal:

- **A:** Jó napot! A nevem Kovács István.
- **B:** Jó napot! Nagy Mária vagyok. Örülök, hogy megismerhetem.
- **A:** Én is örülök. Hogy van?
- **B:** Köszönöm, jól. És ön?
- **A:** Köszönöm szépen, szintén jól vagyok.

## Cultural Notes

::note

**Formality in Hungarian Culture**:

Hungarians take formality in communication seriously. With strangers, elderly people, and in work environments, always use formal address until you are invited to use the informal "te" form.

Prefer using **"ön"**, as **"maga"** can sometimes be perceived as slightly rude in certain situations.

::

::info

**When to Switch to "te" (Informal "you")**:

Usually, young people (students, peers) switch to the informal "te" quickly. With colleagues and older acquaintances, it might take longer. Always wait for the older person or the person of higher status to suggest switching to "te".

::
